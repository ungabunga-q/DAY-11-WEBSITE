Bylaw 68 & 68A: Scope of Maintenance

Society's Duty (68): Maintain all common areas — external structure, terrace, water systems, lifts, staircases, gardens, etc.
Member's Duty (68A): Maintain everything inside their own flat. Crucially, members must cooperate on inter-flat issues like leaks.

Bylaw 69 & 70: Billing and Default

Billing (69): Secretary must ensure timely bill dispatch. Members are responsible for timely payment.
Default (70): Unpaid dues can attract interest (max 21%) *only if* passed in the AGM. After 3 months, a member is a defaulter, and the MC can begin the recovery process (notices, then legal action under Sec 154B-29).

Manager's Pro-Tips

  • Always get the interest rate resolution passed in the first AGM of the financial year. No resolution = no legal right to charge interest.
  • Document everything: Keep records of bill dispatch, payment receipts, and all reminder notices sent to defaulters.

Official Society Business

Bylaws 71 - 73

Bylaw 71: Body Corporate

Upon registration, the society becomes a legal entity. This is critical: all contracts (security, housekeeping, AMC) must be in the society's name, not an individual office bearer's name. This protects individuals from personal liability.

Bylaw 73: The Common Seal

This is the society's official stamp of authority, kept securely by the Secretary. It's used on highly important documents like Share Certificates and the Deed of Conveyance. Its use requires a committee resolution and must be co-signed by the Chairman, Secretary, and an authorized member.

Manager's Pro-Tips

  • Never sign a vendor contract in your personal name, even if you are the Chairman or Secretary. Always use the society's full registered name.
  • Maintain a "Seal Register" to log every instance the Common Seal is used, including the date, document details, and authorizing resolution number.

Bylaw 75: Change of Use

A unit must be used strictly for its intended purpose (e.g., residential for residential flats). Any change, like starting a business, crèche, or extensive tuition classes, requires explicit, prior written permission from the Managing Committee. Verbal approvals are not valid.

Safety & Mandatory Audits

Bylaws 76 & 77

Bylaw 76: Structural Audit

This is a mandatory health check-up for the building's structure.
• Frequency (15-30 yrs old): Every 5 years.
• Frequency (>30 yrs old): Every 3 years.
It must be conducted by an engineer from the official panel of the local Municipal Corporation.

Bylaw 77B: Fire Audit

This is a non-negotiable safety requirement.
• Frequency: Every 6 months.
• Action: Submit Form B report to the Fire Brigade. Keep acknowledgement receipts on file.
• Manager's Role: Ensure the fire-fighting vendor performs checks and submits the report. Also, ensure common areas (lobbies, staircases) are always clear of obstructions like shoe racks or furniture.

Bylaw 76D / 77B: Lift Inspection

Frequency: Annually. This is conducted by a government Lift Inspector from the PWD. The society must coordinate with their lift AMC vendor to be present during the inspection and must pay the requisite government fees.

Parking Rules Explained

Bylaws 78 - 84

Key Distinction

These bylaws apply when the society allots parking. If a builder has sold a parking spot as part of the flat agreement, these rules generally do not override that sale.

Bylaw 78 & 80: Allotment & Eligibility

Allotment is on a 'first come, first serve' basis to members who own a vehicle. Generally, one slot per member. If slots are surplus, they can be allotted for a year to others.

Bylaw 81: Limited Parking Scenario

If there are more cars than parking slots, the society *must* use a fair and transparent method for yearly allotment. The two recommended methods are a Lottery System or a Rotation System.

Bylaw 78B, 83, 84: Usage & Charges

A society-allotted spot cannot be sold or transferred. If the member sells their flat, the spot returns to the society. The member must pay the monthly parking charges (fixed by AGM) regardless of whether they use the spot. These rules also cover two-wheelers.
