Group A: Maintenance, Bills, and Payments

Bylaw Summary

  • Bylaw 68: Society maintains all common areas (structure, water systems, lights, amenities).
  • Bylaw 68A: Members must maintain everything inside their own flats.
  • Bylaw 69: Secretary dispatches bills; members must pay on time.
  • Bylaw 70: Society can charge up to 21% p.a. interest on late payments. Defaulters (over 3 months due) face a recovery process after notices.

Manager's Checklist

FAQ: What if a member refuses to cooperate on an internal leak affecting others? +

The society should first mediate. If the member remains uncooperative, the committee can send a formal letter outlining their responsibility under Bylaw 68A and warning of potential liability for damages.

Group B: Official Society Business & Legal Status

Bylaw Summary

  • Bylaw 71: The society is a 'Body Corporate', a legal entity that can sign contracts.
  • Bylaw 73: The official Common Seal is kept by the Secretary and used on major documents like Share Certificates, authenticated by office bearers.

Group C: Rules for Using Your Property

Bylaw Summary

  • Bylaw 75: Members must use their flat for the purpose specified (e.g., residential). Prior written permission from the MC is required for any change of use.
  • Penalty: Unauthorized change of use can lead to a penalty of 5x the monthly maintenance charge.

Manager's Checklist

Group D: Building Safety & Mandatory Check-ups

Bylaw Summary

  • Bylaw 76 (Structural Audit): Every 5 years for buildings 15-30 years old; every 3 years for buildings over 30. Must be done by a panel-approved engineer.
  • Bylaw 77B (Fire Audit): Mandatory check of all fire systems every 6 months. Form B report must be submitted to the Fire Brigade.
  • Bylaw 76D/77B (Lift Inspection): Annual inspection by the government's Lift Inspector. *(Note: Bylaw number may vary, but the annual requirement is key).*

Manager's Checklist

FAQ: Who is responsible if the society fails a fire audit? +

The entire Managing Committee is held responsible. While a notice to rectify issues is given first, repeated failures can lead to penalties or even cancellation of the Fire NOC, which has serious legal and insurance implications.

Group E: The Complete Guide to Parking Rules

Bylaw Summary

  • Bylaw 78-79: Parking rules are framed by the General Body. Spots must be marked and used only for vehicles.
  • Bylaw 80: Eligibility requires vehicle ownership. Generally one spot per member.
  • Bylaw 81: If spots are limited, a fair lottery or rotation system must be used annually.
  • Bylaw 82-84: Members must apply formally. Charges are mandatory once a spot is allotted. Rules also apply to two-wheelers.
